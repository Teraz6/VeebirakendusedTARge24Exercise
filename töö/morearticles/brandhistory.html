The Legacy of Pashley Cycles

In 1926, in the heart of Stratford-upon-Avon, a small workshop began crafting bicycles that would go on to become symbols of quality and tradition—Pashley Cycles. Founded by William Pashley, the company’s early focus was on producing reliable bikes for everyday riders. By the mid-20th century, Pashley was renowned for its sturdy, handmade bicycles built to withstand both urban and countryside landscapes.

What set Pashley apart was its commitment to craftsmanship. Every frame, wheel, and part was meticulously constructed in England, ensuring that each bicycle was not only functional but a work of art. Pashley became synonymous with elegance and durability, blending classic designs with innovative engineering.

Over the years, the brand expanded, creating iconic models like the Guv’nor and Princess, which captured the essence of British cycling culture. Today, Pashley continues to thrive, producing everything from commuter bikes to high-end vintage-style machines.

While many things have changed, one thing remains the same: Pashley’s dedication to quality, built with pride in England. As a brand that has endured through generations, Pashley Cycles remains a symbol of timeless British engineering, connecting riders to a century of cycling heritage.
