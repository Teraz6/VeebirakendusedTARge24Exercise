On the Road: From the Fixed Gear Fanatic's Point of View

For a fixed gear fanatic, the road is more than just pavement—it's a canvas for pure cycling expression. There's something raw and exhilarating about riding a bike with no gears, no coasting, and no distractions. Just you, your bike, and the rhythm of the pedals.

As I glide through the streets, the city becomes my playground. The fixed gear bike, often called a "fixie," offers a unique connection to the road. Every turn of the pedal is a direct interaction with the ride—there's no freewheel, so I control every moment, every speed, and every stop. It's an unmatched feeling of being in tune with the bike and the environment around me.

Fixed gear riding isn’t about speed or performance—it’s about simplicity and control. Whether I'm navigating the bustling city traffic or cruising along a quiet backstreet, I embrace the challenge of a direct-drive ride. The lack of gears means I’m always engaged, always adjusting, always in the moment.

Other cyclists might pass by, but I know that for me, the fixie is more than a bike—it's a lifestyle, a mindset, and a love letter to the open road.
