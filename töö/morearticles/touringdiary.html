Touring Diary: A Sketchbook in Your Basket

As the wheels of my bike hummed against the quiet countryside road, I couldn’t help but feel a sense of freedom that only a long bike tour could bring. Tucked in the front basket of my bike, alongside my water bottle and snacks, was my trusty sketchbook. It was small, worn around the edges from years of travel, but it had become my companion on every journey.

I stopped by a meadow, the golden sun casting a warm glow across the hills. As I set down my bike and flipped open the sketchbook, the world seemed to slow. The gentle rustle of leaves, the distant call of a bird, and the soft whisper of the wind—all were captured in quick strokes of my pencil.

Each page told the story of my travels—mountain passes, winding rivers, tiny villages with cobbled streets. The sketches weren’t perfect, but they held the essence of my journey, the moments I’d experienced along the way.

As I packed up to continue my ride, I felt a deep sense of gratitude. My sketchbook in the basket was more than a collection of drawings—it was a reminder of the beauty found in the simplicity of the open road.
