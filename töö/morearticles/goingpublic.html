Going Public: Out & About with the Founder of Public

On a sunny afternoon in San Francisco, I joined Rob, the founder of Public Bikes, for a ride through the city. Public, known for its stylish, practical bicycles, started with a simple vision: to make cycling more accessible and enjoyable for everyone. Rob’s enthusiasm for the brand was infectious as we weaved through the city’s iconic streets.

As we pedaled past colorful storefronts and bustling neighborhoods, Rob shared his journey from the early days of designing bicycles to creating a community around them. "We wanted bikes that were both functional and beautiful," he explained, "something you could ride to work and then take out for a leisurely weekend cruise."

Public’s emphasis on simplicity and comfort resonated with cyclists of all kinds, from city commuters to weekend explorers. Rob also pointed out how the brand's commitment to sustainability helped make cycling a more eco-friendly alternative to cars.

As we stopped for coffee, Rob reflected on how Public had grown into a movement that encouraged people to embrace cycling not just as a mode of transportation but as a way of life. Our ride ended with a sense of shared purpose—a belief that bikes, at their core, are about freedom, community, and enjoying the world around us.
